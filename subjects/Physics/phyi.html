🔥 Practice MCQs (VECTORS)

1. Two forces of 7 N and 24 N act at an angle of 90° to each other. What is the magnitude of their resultant?

A) 25 N B) 17 N C) 31 N D) 13 N

✅ Correct Answer: A) 25 N

Solution:

Why we are using pythagoras therom here? Because here two parameters given and the angle is 90°, which means that triangle has one angle is 90°.
Use the Pythagorean Theorem: R = √(7² + 24²) = √(49 + 576) = √625 = 25 N
📌 Concept: Vector addition using right angle rule.

2. If the dot product of two vectors A and B is zero, and their magnitudes are non-zero, what is the angle between them?

A) 0° B) 45° C) 90° D) 180°

✅ Correct Answer: C) 90°

Solution:

TRICK: DOT PRODUCT: A · B = ABcosθ dot product is given zero means: A · B = 0
magnitudes of vectors are non zero means: AB ≠ 0
A · B = ABcosθ ==> 0 = ABcosθ ==> cos-1(0) = AB ==> 90° = AB
Cos(90) = 0, so Cos-1(0) = 90°

3. A vector of magnitude 10 units makes an angle of 60° with the x-axis. What is the Y-component of the vector?

A) 5 units B) 8.66 units C) 10 units D) 6.5 units

✅ Correct Answer: B) 8.66 units

Solution:

TRICK: Question have only one vector and with given x-y components, it means that angle is always 60, We just have to check what to apply Sin or Cos
Formulas:
Ax = Acosθ and Ay = Asinθ
Here we will use Ay = Asinθ because we are given to find the Y-Component
So, Ay = 10Sin(60) = 10(1/√2) = 8.66 units
📌 Concept: Trigonometric resolution into components.

4. Let A = 3i + 2j and B = i − 4j. What is A × B?

A) −14k B) 14k C) 10k D) −10k

✅ Correct Answer: A) −14k

Solution:

A × B = (3×−4 − 2×1)k = (−12 − 2)k = −14k
📌 Concept: Cross product via determinant.

5. If A · B = ABcosθ and θ = 180°, what is the result of dot product?

A) AB B) -AB C) 0 D) Undefined

✅ Correct Answer: B) -AB

Solution:

cos(180°) = -1 ⇒ A · B = AB × (−1) = −AB
📌 Concept: Negative dot product for opposite vectors.

6. What is the unit vector of A = 6i − 8j?

A) (6i − 8j)/10 B) (6i + 8j)/14 C) (3i − 4j)/5 D) (3i + 4j)/5

✅ Correct Answer: A) (6i − 8j)/10

Solution:

|A| = √(6² + 8²) = √100 = 10 ⇒ Unit vector = A / |A| = (6i − 8j)/10
📌 Concept: Vector normalization.

7. Two equal vectors are added. For what angle between them is the resultant minimum?

A) 0° B) 60° C) 90° D) 180°

✅ Correct Answer: D) 180°

Solution:

R = √(A² + A² + 2A²cosθ); R is minimum when cosθ = −1 ⇒ θ = 180°
📌 Concept: Min/max resultant based on angle.

8. If two vectors are collinear, then their cross product is:

A) Zero vector B) Unit vector C) Scalar D) Undefined

✅ Correct Answer: A) Zero vector

Solution:

A × B = AB sinθ; if θ = 0° or 180° ⇒ sinθ = 0 ⇒ A × B = 0
📌 Concept: Cross product of collinear vectors is zero.

9. If a vector A = 4i + 3j and vector B = 2i − j, what is the angle between them?

A) 30° B) 45° C) 60° D) 90°

✅ Correct Answer: C) 60°

Solution:

A · B = |A||B|cosθ
A · B = (4)(2) + (3)(−1) = 8 − 3 = 5
|A| = √(16 + 9) = √25 = 5, |B| = √(4 + 1) = √5
⇒ cosθ = 5 / (5√5) = 1/√5
⇒ θ = cos−1(1/√5) ≈ 63.4° ≈ 60°
📌 Concept: Angle via dot product & magnitude.

10. A vector makes an angle of 45° with both the x and y axes. What is the magnitude of its resultant component in the XY-plane if its magnitude is 10?

A) 7.07 B) 14.14 C) 5 D) 10

✅ Correct Answer: A) 7.07

Solution:

Components: Ax = 10cos45° = 10(1/√2) = 7.07, Ay = 10sin45° = 7.07
Resultant in XY-plane: R = √(Ax² + Ay²) = √(50 + 50) = √100 = 10 (full vector)
But each component is 7.07
📌 Concept: Equal angle resolution.

11. Which of the following is NOT a valid vector operation?

A) Addition of two vectors B) Scalar multiplication of a vector C) Division of a vector by another vector D) Projection of a vector onto another

✅ Correct Answer: C) Division of a vector by another vector

Solution:

Division of vectors is undefined in vector algebra. There is no such operation. Others are valid operations.
📌 Concept: Valid vector algebra operations.

12. A particle moves 4 m north, then 3 m east. What is the magnitude of its displacement?

A) 7 m B) 5 m C) 1 m D) 6 m

✅ Correct Answer: B) 5 m

Solution:

Displacement = √(4² + 3²) = √(16 + 9) = √25 = 5 m
📌 Concept: Pythagorean displacement in 2D motion.

13. What is the angle between two vectors if their dot product is negative?

A) Less than 90° B) 90° C) Greater than 90° D) Zero

✅ Correct Answer: C) Greater than 90°

Solution:

Dot product A · B = ABcosθ. For a negative result, cosθ < 0, which happens when θ > 90°.
📌 Concept: Interpretation of sign in scalar (dot) product.

14. If vector A = i + 2j and vector B = 2i + 3j, what is A · B?

A) 8 B) 7 C) 6 D) 5

✅ Correct Answer: A) 8

Solution:

A · B = (1)(2) + (2)(3) = 2 + 6 = 8
📌 Concept: Dot product of 2D vectors.

15. A force of 10 N acts at an angle of 30° to the horizontal. What is the horizontal component?

A) 5 N B) 8.66 N C) 10 N D) 6.32 N

✅ Correct Answer: B) 8.66 N

Solution:

Fx = Fcosθ = 10cos(30°) = 10 × √3/2 ≈ 8.66 N
📌 Concept: Vector component resolution.

16. Which of the following is not a vector quantity?

A) Displacement B) Velocity C) Work D) Acceleration

✅ Correct Answer: C) Work

Solution:

Work is a scalar quantity because it has only magnitude, no direction.
📌 Concept: Distinguishing scalar vs vector.

17. What is the result of A × A where A is any vector?

A) |A|² B) 0 C) A D) Undefined

✅ Correct Answer: B) 0

Solution:

A × A = 0 because the angle between a vector and itself is zero, and sin(0) = 0.
📌 Concept: Self-cross product.

18. Which of the following pairs of vectors have a maximum cross product?

A) Parallel vectors B) Perpendicular vectors C) Anti-parallel vectors D) Zero vectors

✅ Correct Answer: B) Perpendicular vectors

Solution:

Cross product is maximum when sinθ = 1, i.e., θ = 90° (perpendicular).
📌 Concept: Maximum value of vector cross product.

19. What is the projection of vector A = 3i + 4j onto B = 5i?

A) 5 B) 4 C) √25 D) 3

✅ Correct Answer: D) 3

Solution:

Projection = (A·B) / |B| = (3×5)/5 = 15/5 = 3
📌 Concept: Vector projection on another vector.

20. What is the unit vector from P(1, 2, 3) to Q(4, 6, 3)?

A) (1/5)i + (2/5)j B) (3/5)i + (4/5)j C) (3/6)i + (4/6)j D) (1/√5)i + (2/√5)j

✅ Correct Answer: B) (3/5)i + (4/5)j

Solution:

Vector PQ = Q − P = (3, 4, 0), magnitude = 5, unit vector = (3/5)i + (4/5)j
📌 Concept: Unit vector from one point to another.

21. Which is the correct parametric vector form of a line through (1,2,0) with direction (3,1,−1)?

A) r = i + 2j − k + t(3i + j − k) B) r = i + 2j + t(3i + j − k) C) r = 3ti + tj − tk D) r = i + 2j + k + t(i + 2j − k)

✅ Correct Answer: B) r = i + 2j + t(3i + j − k)

Solution:

r = position vector + t(direction) ⇒ (1,2,0) + t(3,1,−1)
📌 Concept: Vector form of a line.

22. Find |A × B| for A = i + 2j + 3k, B = 3i + j + k

A) √26 B) √29 C) √30 D) √90

✅ Correct Answer: D) √90

Solution:

A × B = −i + 8j − 5k ⇒ Magnitude = √(1² + 8² + 5²) = √90
📌 Concept: Cross product magnitude.

23. If A = i + j, B = j + k, C = k + i, then A·(B×C) = ?

A) 2 B) 1 C) 0 D) −1

✅ Correct Answer: C) 0

Solution:

A·(B×C) = scalar triple product. B×C = −j + k; A·(−j + k) = 0
📌 Concept: Scalar triple product = 0 ⇒ coplanar.

24. Angle between vectors A = 2i + 3j and B = 4i + j is:

A) 0° B) 45° C) ≈ 46.4° D) 90°

✅ Correct Answer: C) ≈ 46.4°

Solution:

cosθ = A·B / |A||B| = 11 / (√13 × √17) ⇒ θ ≈ 46.4°
📌 Concept: Dot product & angle between vectors.

25. What is the area of the triangle formed by vectors A = 2i + j and B = i − j?

A) 1 unit² B) 1.5 unit² C) 2 unit² D) 3 unit²

✅ Correct Answer: B) 1.5 unit²

Solution:

Area = 0.5 × |A × B| = 0.5 × 3 = 1.5
📌 Concept: Triangle area from two vectors.

🔥 Practice MCQs (MOTION AND FORCE)

1. A body moves with uniform acceleration. Which graph best represents its velocity-time relation?

A) A straight line parallel to time axis B) A curved line C) A straight line with positive slope D) A vertical line

✅ Correct Answer: C) A straight line with positive slope

Solution:

Velocity increases linearly in uniform acceleration ⇒ v–t graph is a sloped straight line.
📌 Concept: Linear motion with constant acceleration.

2. What is the SI unit of force?

A) Joule B) Kilogram C) Newton D) Watt

✅ Correct Answer: C) Newton

Solution:

1 Newton = 1 kg·m/s² — derived from F = ma.
📌 Concept: Force units in SI system.

3. Which law explains the recoil of a gun when a bullet is fired?

A) Newton’s First Law B) Newton’s Second Law C) Newton’s Third Law D) Law of Gravitation

✅ Correct Answer: C) Newton’s Third Law

Solution:

Action: Bullet moves forward. Reaction: Gun recoils backward.
📌 Concept: Every action has an equal and opposite reaction.

4. A 5 kg object is acted upon by a net force of 20 N. What is its acceleration?

A) 4 m/s² B) 5 m/s² C) 25 m/s² D) 100 m/s²

✅ Correct Answer: A) 4 m/s²

Solution:

F = ma ⇒ a = F/m = 20/5 = 4 m/s²
📌 Concept: Newton's 2nd Law.

5. Which quantity has both magnitude and direction but is not a force?

A) Work B) Velocity C) Energy D) Mass

✅ Correct Answer: B) Velocity

Solution:

Velocity is a vector quantity like force, but it represents speed with direction — not a force.
📌 Concept: Vector vs Scalar quantities.

6. What force is required to keep a 10 kg object moving at constant velocity?

A) 10 N B) 0 N C) 9.8 N D) 98 N

✅ Correct Answer: B) 0 N

Solution:

If velocity is constant, acceleration = 0 ⇒ Net force = 0 (Newton’s 1st Law).
📌 Concept: Inertia and balanced forces.

7. Which of the following represents the correct equation of motion?

A) v = u + at B) s = ut + ½at² C) v² = u² + 2as D) All of the above

✅ Correct Answer: D) All of the above

Solution:

All three are standard equations of uniformly accelerated motion.
📌 Concept: Kinematic equations in linear motion.

8. A ball is thrown vertically upward with an initial velocity of 50 m/s. The time it takes to reach the maximum height is:

A) 2.5 s B) 5 s C) 10 s D) 25 s

✅ Correct Answer: B) 5 s

Solution:

Time to reach max height is given by:
t = v / g = 50 / 10 = 5 s.
📌 Concept: Motion under gravity.

9. A car accelerates uniformly from 10 m/s to 30 m/s over 100 m. What is its acceleration?

A) 2 m/s² B) 3 m/s² C) 4 m/s² D) 5 m/s²

✅ Correct Answer: A) 2 m/s²

Solution:

Using v² = u² + 2as → 30² = 10² + 2a(100) ⇒ a = 2 m/s².
📌 Concept: Kinematic equation.

10. A 4 kg object is moving with velocity 6 m/s. A constant force acts for 3 seconds and increases its velocity to 18 m/s. What is the magnitude of the force?

A) 12 N B) 14 N C) 16 N D) 18 N

✅ Correct Answer: C) 16 N

Solution:

F = m × a = m × (Δv / t) = 4 × (18−6)/3 = 16 N.
📌 Concept: Newton’s 2nd law & impulse.

11. A projectile is fired at an angle θ with the horizontal. If the range is maximum, what is the value of θ?

A) 30° B) 45° C) 60° D) 90°

✅ Correct Answer: B) 45°

Solution:

Range is maximum when sin(2θ) = 1, so 2θ = 90° → θ = 45°.
📌 Concept: Projectile motion – maximum range condition.

12. A 5 kg block rests on a frictionless horizontal surface. A force of 20 N is applied. What is the acceleration?

A) 2 m/s² B) 3 m/s² C) 4 m/s² D) 5 m/s²

✅ Correct Answer: D) 5 m/s²

Solution:

F = ma ⇒ a = F/m = 20/5 = 4 m/s².
📌 Concept: Newton’s Second Law (F = ma).

13. A car accelerates from rest at a constant rate. Which of the following graphs best represents velocity vs. time?

A) Straight line with negative slope B) Straight line with positive slope C) Exponential curve D) Horizontal line

✅ Correct Answer: B) Straight line with positive slope

Solution:

Constant acceleration means uniform increase in velocity. Velocity vs. time graph is a straight line with positive slope.
📌 Concept: Graphical interpretation of uniformly accelerated motion.

14. A body moving in a circle with constant speed has:

A) Constant velocity B) Constant acceleration C) Constant speed but changing velocity D) Zero acceleration

✅ Correct Answer: C) Constant speed but changing velocity

Solution:

In circular motion, direction of velocity changes continuously; so velocity changes though speed is constant.
📌 Concept: Uniform circular motion.

15. If a body is thrown vertically upwards with velocity u, what is the total time to return to ground?

A) u/g B) 2u/g C) u²/2g D) u²/g

✅ Correct Answer: B) 2u/g

Solution:

Time to reach max height = u/g. Total time = 2 × u/g.
📌 Concept: Free fall motion under gravity.

16. A bullet of mass 20 g is fired with velocity 300 m/s into a wooden block of mass 980 g. The final velocity of the system after impact is:

A) 6 m/s B) 3 m/s C) 1.5 m/s D) 2.4 m/s

✅ Correct Answer: B) 3 m/s

Solution:

Conservation of momentum: (0.02×300) = (0.02+0.98)v ⇒ 6 = v ⇒ v = 3 m/s
📌 Concept: Perfectly inelastic collision.

17. Which of the following is not a vector quantity?

A) Impulse B) Force C) Power D) Momentum

✅ Correct Answer: C) Power

Solution:

Power is a scalar, while force, impulse, and momentum have both magnitude and direction.
📌 Concept: Scalars vs Vectors.

18. An object is thrown upward. At the highest point, which of the following is true?

A) Velocity = 0, Acceleration = 0 B) Velocity ≠ 0, Acceleration = 0 C) Velocity = 0, Acceleration = g D) Velocity = g, Acceleration = 0

✅ Correct Answer: C) Velocity = 0, Acceleration = g

Solution:

Velocity becomes zero momentarily at peak; gravity (acceleration) always acts downward.
📌 Concept: Vertical projectile motion.

19. The area under the velocity-time graph gives:

A) Acceleration B) Distance C) Velocity D) Displacement

✅ Correct Answer: D) Displacement

Solution:

Area under v–t graph gives displacement (or distance if motion is in one direction).
📌 Concept: Graphical kinematics.

20. A force acts for 0.1 s on a body of mass 5 kg. It produces a change in velocity of 2 m/s. The magnitude of force is:

A) 50 N B) 100 N C) 150 N D) 200 N

✅ Correct Answer: A) 100 N

Solution:

Impulse = Change in momentum = F × t ⇒ F = Δp / t = (5×2)/0.1 = 100 N
📌 Concept: Impulse-momentum theorem.

21. What is the unit of impulse in SI?

A) N B) Ns C) kg D) m/s

✅ Correct Answer: B) Ns

Solution:

Impulse = Force × time; unit = Newton-second (Ns).
📌 Concept: Dimensional analysis of impulse.

22. Two objects A and B collide elastically. Which physical quantity is always conserved?

A) Kinetic energy B) Momentum C) Both A and B D) None

✅ Correct Answer: C) Both A and B

Solution:

In elastic collisions, both kinetic energy and momentum are conserved.
📌 Concept: Collision types.

23. What is the relation between linear velocity (v) and angular velocity (ω) for circular motion?

A) v = ωr B) v = ω/r C) v = r/ω D) v = ω²r

✅ Correct Answer: A) v = ωr

Solution:

Linear velocity is the product of angular velocity and radius.
📌 Concept: Circular motion kinematics.

24. Which law explains the recoil of a gun when a bullet is fired?

A) Newton’s First Law B) Newton’s Second Law C) Newton’s Third Law D) Law of Gravitation

✅ Correct Answer: C) Newton’s Third Law

Solution:

Action: bullet goes forward. Reaction: gun moves backward. Newton's third law.
📌 Concept: Action-Reaction forces.

25. A block of mass 10 kg is pulled along a rough horizontal surface with a force of 100 N at an angle of 30° above the horizontal. The coefficient of kinetic friction is 0.4. What is the acceleration of the block?

A) 2.3 m/s² B) 3.5 m/s² C) 4.6 m/s² D) 5.0 m/s²

✅ Correct Answer: A) 2.3 m/s²

Solution:

Resolve force: Fx = 100cos(30°), Fy = 100sin(30°)
Normal force: N = mg − Fy = 100 − 50 = 50 N
Friction = μN = 0.4 × 50 = 20 N
Net force = Fx − friction = ~86.6 − 20 = 66.6 N
Acceleration = F / m = 66.6 / 10 ≈ 2.3 m/s²
📌 Concept: Forces on an inclined pull, vector resolution, kinetic friction.

🔥 Practice MCQs (WORK AND ENERGY)

1. Which of the following statements is correct regarding work done?

A) Work is always positive B) Work is always negative C) Work is scalar and can be positive, negative or zero D) Work is a vector

✅ Correct Answer: C) Work is scalar and can be positive, negative or zero

Solution:

Work depends on the angle between force and displacement; hence it can be positive, negative or zero. Work is a scalar quantity.

2. A body of mass 2 kg is lifted vertically through 10 m. What is the work done against gravity?

A) 10 J B) 20 J C) 196 J D) 100 J

✅ Correct Answer: C) 196 J

Solution:

Work = mgh = 2 × 9.8 × 10 = 196 J

3. Which physical quantity is the rate of doing work?

A) Energy B) Force C) Power D) Momentum

✅ Correct Answer: C) Power

Solution:

Power is defined as the rate at which work is done. Power = Work / Time.

4. A body is moving in a circular path with constant speed. What can be said about its work done by centripetal force?

A) Positive B) Negative C) Zero D) Depends on radius

✅ Correct Answer: C) Zero

Solution:

Work is zero when force is perpendicular to displacement. Since centripetal force is always perpendicular to velocity, it does no work.

5. A body of mass 2 kg falls freely from a height of 5 m. The work done by gravity is:

A) 10 J B) 50 J C) 100 J D) Zero

✅ Correct Answer: B) 50 J

Solution:

Work done = mgh = 2 × 10 × 5 = 100 J (gravitational potential energy lost = work done). But negative height direction = downward → work done by gravity = +50 J.

6. A spring of constant k = 200 N/m is compressed 10 cm. What is the stored potential energy?

A) 0.5 J B) 1 J C) 2 J D) 5 J

✅ Correct Answer: B) 1 J

Solution:

PE = ½ kx² = ½ × 200 × (0.1)² = 1 J

7. If the power of a machine is doubled, the work done in the same time will be:

A) Halved B) Same C) Doubled D) Four times

✅ Correct Answer: C) Doubled

Solution:

Power = Work / Time → If Power is doubled and time is constant, Work must be doubled too.

8. The unit of energy in CGS system is:

A) Joule B) Calorie C) Erg D) Watt

✅ Correct Answer: C) Erg

Solution:

In CGS system, unit of energy = erg. 1 erg = 10-7 joules.

9. A 10 kg object is lifted vertically by 5 meters. What is the work done against gravity?

A) 490 J B) 50 J C) 100 J D) 500 J

✅ Correct Answer: A) 490 J

Solution:

Work = mgh = 10 × 9.8 × 5 = 490 J

10. Which form of energy is stored in a compressed gas?

A) Chemical energy B) Kinetic energy C) Potential energy D) Thermal energy

✅ Correct Answer: C) Potential energy

Solution:

The energy due to compression (deformation) is a form of elastic potential energy.

11. A machine does 500 J of work in 10 seconds. What is its power output?

A) 5 W B) 50 W C) 100 W D) 250 W

✅ Correct Answer: B) 50 W

Solution:

Power = Work / Time = 500 / 10 = 50 W

12. Which of the following forces is non-conservative?

A) Gravitational force B) Electrostatic force C) Magnetic force D) Frictional force

✅ Correct Answer: D) Frictional force

Solution:

Friction depends on path; it dissipates energy as heat → hence, it is non-conservative.

13. Which of the following statements about kinetic energy is FALSE?

A) It depends on mass and speed. B) It is always positive. C) It is a vector quantity. D) It can be converted into potential energy.

✅ Correct Answer: C) It is a vector quantity.

Solution:

Kinetic energy is a scalar quantity (has magnitude only, no direction).

14. A body is thrown vertically upward. At the highest point, which form(s) of energy does it possess?

A) Only kinetic energy B) Only potential energy C) Both kinetic and potential energy D) Zero energy

✅ Correct Answer: B) Only potential energy

Solution:

At the highest point, velocity becomes zero, hence KE = 0. Only PE remains.

15. In which scenario is **no work** done by a force?

A) Force is applied, and body moves in its direction B) Force is applied perpendicular to displacement C) Force is applied opposite to motion D) Force causes rotation

✅ Correct Answer: B) Force is applied perpendicular to displacement

Solution:

Work = F × d × cos(θ); if θ = 90°, then cos(90°) = 0 → No work done.

16. A spring with constant k is compressed by x. What is the stored energy?

A) kx B) kx² C) ½kx D) ½kx²

✅ Correct Answer: D) ½kx²

Solution:

Elastic potential energy = ½ kx²

17. Which of these units is NOT a unit of energy?

A) Joule B) Calorie C) Watt D) Electron-volt

✅ Correct Answer: C) Watt

Solution:

Watt is unit of power (J/s), not energy. Energy units include J, cal, eV.

18. The work-energy theorem is represented by:

A) W = ΔV B) W = ΔU C) W = ΔKE D) W = ΔPE

✅ Correct Answer: C) W = ΔKE

Solution:

The work done by the net force on an object is equal to the change in its kinetic energy.

19. A boy pushes a wall but it does not move. What is the work done?

A) Positive B) Negative C) Zero D) Infinite

✅ Correct Answer: C) Zero

Solution:

Since displacement = 0, work = force × 0 = 0

20. Which of the following is true for a conservative force?

A) Depends on path taken B) Always positive C) Work done is path-independent D) Depends only on speed

✅ Correct Answer: C) Work done is path-independent

Solution:

For conservative forces (like gravity, spring), only initial and final positions matter.

21. Power is defined as:

A) Force × Distance B) Work × Time C) Work ÷ Time D) Energy × Displacement

✅ Correct Answer: C) Work ÷ Time

Solution:

Power = Work / Time; unit: Watt (W)

22. If a body gains kinetic energy, the work done on it is:

A) Zero B) Negative C) Positive D) Infinite

✅ Correct Answer: C) Positive

Solution:

Gain in kinetic energy implies work is done *on* the body → positive work.

23. What happens to the kinetic energy if the speed of a body is tripled?

A) It doubles B) It becomes nine times C) It triples D) It becomes six times

✅ Correct Answer: B) It becomes nine times

Solution:

Kinetic energy ∝ v². So, if speed becomes 3v, KE becomes (3)² = 9 times.

24. Which of the following forces is always non-conservative?

A) Gravitational force B) Spring force C) Frictional force D) Electrostatic force

✅ Correct Answer: C) Frictional force

Solution:

Friction always dissipates mechanical energy → path-dependent → non-conservative.

25. An object of mass 4 kg moves with velocity 5 m/s. What is its kinetic energy?

A) 10 J B) 25 J C) 50 J D) 100 J

✅ Correct Answer: C) 50 J

Solution:

KE = ½mv² = ½ × 4 × 25 = 50 J.

🔥 Practice MCQs (ANGULAR/CIRCULAR MOTION)

1. What is the angular displacement when a wheel rotates 3 full revolutions?

A) 2π rad B) 6π rad C) 3π rad D) 12π rad

✅ Correct Answer: B) 6π rad

Solution:

One full revolution = 2π rad, so 3 revolutions = 6π rad.

2. The SI unit of angular velocity is:

A) rad/s B) rev/min C) m/s D) Hz

✅ Correct Answer: A) rad/s

Solution:

Angular velocity is measured in radians per second (rad/s).

3. Which physical quantity remains conserved in circular motion when no external torque is applied?

A) Linear momentum B) Angular momentum C) Kinetic energy D) Acceleration

✅ Correct Answer: B) Angular momentum

Solution:

In absence of external torque, angular momentum is conserved.

4. Centripetal force always acts:

A) Tangent to the path B) Outward from the center C) Toward the center D) Perpendicular to radius

✅ Correct Answer: C) Toward the center

Solution:

Centripetal force always points toward the center of the circular path.

5. What is the unit of angular acceleration in SI system?

A) rad/s B) rad/s² C) m/s² D) m/s

✅ Correct Answer: B) rad/s²

Solution:

Angular acceleration is the rate of change of angular velocity. Its unit is radians per second squared (rad/s²).

6. A body is moving in a circle of radius 2 m with a speed of 4 m/s. What is its centripetal acceleration?

A) 4 m/s² B) 6 m/s² C) 8 m/s² D) 12 m/s²

✅ Correct Answer: C) 8 m/s²

Solution:

Centripetal acceleration = v²/r = (4²)/2 = 16/2 = 8 m/s².

7. What is the angular displacement in radians when a wheel completes one full revolution?

A) 2π B) π C) 4π D) π/2

✅ Correct Answer: A) 2π

Solution:

One full revolution corresponds to 360°, which is equal to 2π radians.

8. Which of the following is NOT a vector quantity in circular motion?

A) Angular velocity B) Angular acceleration C) Angular displacement D) Angular frequency

✅ Correct Answer: D) Angular frequency

Solution:

Angular frequency is a scalar quantity; it only has magnitude, not direction.

9. Tangential velocity is maximum when:

A) Radius is minimum B) Radius is maximum C) Angular velocity is zero D) Angular acceleration is minimum

✅ Correct Answer: B) Radius is maximum

Solution:

Tangential velocity v = ωr. If angular velocity is constant, v increases with radius.

10. What happens to the centripetal force if the speed of the object doubles in circular motion?

A) Doubles B) Triples C) Quadruples D) Remains same

✅ Correct Answer: C) Quadruples

Solution:

F = mv²/r, so if v doubles, F becomes 4 times (since v² term).

11. A disc rotates from rest with a constant angular acceleration. What is the angular velocity after time t?

A) ω = αt B) ω = α/t C) ω = 2αt D) ω = αt²

✅ Correct Answer: A) ω = αt

Solution:

Using angular kinematics equation: ω = ω₀ + αt, and since ω₀ = 0, we get ω = αt.

12. In uniform circular motion, which of the following remains constant?

A) Speed B) Velocity C) Direction D) Acceleration

✅ Correct Answer: A) Speed

Solution:

In uniform circular motion, the speed is constant but direction (and hence velocity) changes continuously.

13. A particle is moving in a circle with uniform angular velocity. Which of the following is true about the net work done over one complete revolution?

A) Equal to kinetic energy B) Equal to angular momentum C) Zero D) Maximum at end

✅ Correct Answer: C) Zero

Solution:

Since there is no change in kinetic energy and the force is perpendicular to displacement in circular motion, work done over one revolution is zero.

14. A flywheel rotating with angular velocity ω is brought to rest by a constant torque τ in time t. What is its moment of inertia I?

A) I = τt / ω B) I = τω / t C) I = tω / τ D) I = τ / ωt

✅ Correct Answer: A) I = τt / ω

Solution:

Using the equation τ = Iα and α = ω/t, we get: I = τ / α = τt / ω.

15. If a rotating body’s angular velocity is doubled and its moment of inertia is halved, what happens to its rotational kinetic energy?

A) Doubled B) Quadrupled C) Same D) Halved

✅ Correct Answer: B) Quadrupled

Solution:

K.E. = (1/2) I ω². If ω → 2ω and I → I/2, then K.E. becomes (1/2)(I/2)(4ω²) = (Iω²), which is double the original energy.

16. A disc is rotating with angular velocity ω. If its moment of inertia is doubled while keeping angular momentum conserved, what will be the new angular velocity?

A) ω/4 B) ω/2 C) 2ω D) ω

✅ Correct Answer: B) ω/2

Solution:

Angular momentum L = Iω. If L is conserved and I becomes 2I, then ω must become ω/2.

17. Which of the following has the greatest moment of inertia about its central axis?

A) Solid sphere B) Solid cylinder C) Thin circular ring D) Disc

✅ Correct Answer: C) Thin circular ring

Solution:

Moment of inertia is highest when mass is farthest from the axis. For ring, I = MR² which is greater than other given objects.

18. An object rotating at 5 rad/s comes to rest in 10 seconds. Assuming uniform angular deceleration, what is the angular displacement?

A) 10 rad B) 25 rad C) 50 rad D) 100 rad

✅ Correct Answer: B) 25 rad

Solution:

Use formula θ = (ω₀ × t) / 2 = (5 × 10) / 2 = 25 rad.

19. A rotating flywheel has an angular acceleration of 2 rad/s². If it starts from rest, what is its angular velocity after 8 seconds?

A) 8 rad/s B) 12 rad/s C) 16 rad/s D) 20 rad/s

✅ Correct Answer: C) 16 rad/s

Solution:

Angular velocity ω = α × t = 2 × 8 = 16 rad/s.

20. Which of the following conditions must be satisfied for pure rolling motion of a wheel?

A) v = rα B) v = ωr C) a = αr D) None of these

✅ Correct Answer: B) v = ωr

Solution:

For pure rolling, the linear velocity v of the center equals the tangential velocity at the rim: v = ωr.

21. A particle moves in a circle of radius r with constant speed. Which of the following statements is true about the direction of acceleration?

A) Tangential to the path B) Radially outward C) Radially inward D) Perpendicular to radius

✅ Correct Answer: C) Radially inward

Solution:

In uniform circular motion, centripetal acceleration is always directed radially inward toward the center of the circle.

22. A rotating object completes 5 revolutions in 2 seconds. What is its angular velocity in rad/s?

A) 2π B) 5π C) 10π D) 20π

✅ Correct Answer: C) 10π

Solution:

ω = θ / t = (5 × 2π) / 2 = 10π / 2 = 10π rad/s

23. A body of mass m tied to a string is whirled in a circle. The string suddenly breaks. The body will:

A) Move in a circle of larger radius B) Move in a circle of smaller radius C) Move tangentially to the circular path D) Fall vertically downward

✅ Correct Answer: C) Move tangentially to the circular path

Solution:

Once the string breaks, no centripetal force acts. The body follows tangential path due to inertia (Newton's First Law).

24. Which physical quantity is conserved when a skater pulls in their arms during a spin?

A) Linear velocity B) Angular velocity C) Angular momentum D) Moment of inertia

✅ Correct Answer: C) Angular momentum

Solution:

In the absence of external torque, angular momentum is conserved. Pulling arms in decreases moment of inertia and increases angular velocity.

25. A solid sphere and a hollow sphere roll down the same incline without slipping. Which reaches the bottom first and why?

A) Hollow sphere, because of higher inertia B) Solid sphere, because it has less moment of inertia C) Both reach at the same time D) None of these

✅ Correct Answer: B) Solid sphere, because it has less moment of inertia

Solution:

Solid sphere accelerates more due to lower I/mR² ratio compared to a hollow sphere, so it reaches first when rolling without slipping.

🔥 Practice MCQs (FLUID PHYSICS)

1. A submarine increases its depth in seawater. What happens to the pressure it experiences?

A) Decreases linearly B) Remains constant C) Increases linearly with depth D) Increases exponentially

✅ Correct Answer: C) Increases linearly with depth

Solution:

Pressure in a fluid is given by the formula: P = P₀ + ρgh, where P₀ is atmospheric pressure, ρ is the fluid density, g is gravitational acceleration, and h is the depth. Since pressure increases directly with depth, it increases linearly as the submarine goes deeper.

2. A block of wood floats in water and also in oil. In which fluid does it experience more buoyant force?

A) Oil B) Water C) Same in both D) Depends on temperature

✅ Correct Answer: B) Water

Solution:

Buoyant force equals the weight of fluid displaced. Since water is denser than oil, it provides more upward force, so the block experiences greater buoyant force in water.

3. Bernoulli's principle is based on the conservation of:

A) Energy B) Momentum C) Mass D) Charge

✅ Correct Answer: A) Energy

Solution:

Bernoulli’s principle says the total mechanical energy of a flowing fluid (pressure energy + kinetic energy + potential energy) remains constant. The equation is: P + ½ρv² + ρgh = constant.

4. Which of the following devices operates based on Pascal’s law?

A) Barometer B) Hydraulic press C) Venturimeter D) Thermometer

✅ Correct Answer: B) Hydraulic press

Solution:

Pascal’s law states that when pressure is applied to a fluid in a closed system, it is transmitted equally in all directions. This principle is used in hydraulic systems to lift heavy loads.

5. What is the SI unit of dynamic viscosity?

A) Pa·s B) N·s/m² C) kg/(m·s) D) All of the above

✅ Correct Answer: D) All of the above

Solution:

Dynamic viscosity can be expressed in multiple equivalent SI units: Pascal-seconds (Pa·s), Newton-seconds per square meter (N·s/m²), and kilograms per meter per second (kg/m·s). All mean the same thing.

6. A small spherical ball is falling through a liquid with a constant velocity. What can be said about the net force acting on it?

A) It is equal to the weight of the ball B) It is zero C) It is equal to the upthrust D) It is equal to the viscous force

✅ Correct Answer: B) It is zero

Solution:

Since the ball is falling at a constant velocity, all the forces acting on it are balanced. This means the net force is zero — the weight is balanced by the sum of buoyant force and viscous drag.

7. The pressure at a depth h in a fluid of density ρ is given by:

A) ρgh B) gh/ρ C) h/ρg D) ρ/g

✅ Correct Answer: A) ρgh

Solution:

Pressure due to a static fluid at a depth h is calculated using the formula P = ρgh, where ρ is the fluid density, g is acceleration due to gravity, and h is the depth.

8. The terminal velocity of a small spherical object falling through a viscous fluid depends on:

A) Radius of the object B) Viscosity of the fluid C) Density difference between object and fluid D) All of the above

✅ Correct Answer: D) All of the above

Solution:

Terminal velocity depends on the radius of the object, the fluid's viscosity, and the density difference between the object and the fluid, as given by Stokes’ law.

9. The rise of a liquid in a capillary tube is due to:

A) Viscosity B) Surface tension C) Atmospheric pressure D) Gravity

✅ Correct Answer: B) Surface tension

Solution:

Capillary action happens because of surface tension and adhesive forces between the liquid and the tube. This allows liquids to rise or fall in narrow tubes without any external force.

10. The unit of viscosity in the SI system is:

A) Pa·s B) N·s/m² C) kg/(m·s) D) All of the above

✅ Correct Answer: D) All of the above

Solution:

All listed units are equivalent representations of viscosity in SI. 1 Pa·s = 1 N·s/m² = 1 kg/(m·s).

11. Which of the following is a correct statement about streamline flow?

A) The flow is irregular B) The velocity at a point changes with time C) Each particle moves along a definite path D) The direction of flow changes abruptly

✅ Correct Answer: C) Each particle moves along a definite path

Solution:

In streamline (laminar) flow, fluid particles follow smooth, fixed paths, and the flow is orderly without sudden changes in velocity or direction.

12. Bernoulli’s principle is based on the conservation of:

A) Mass B) Momentum C) Energy D) Volume

✅ Correct Answer: C) Energy

Solution:

Bernoulli’s theorem states that the total mechanical energy (pressure energy + kinetic energy + potential energy) of the fluid remains constant along a streamline.

13. A hydraulic lift works on the principle of:

A) Newton’s third law B) Pascal’s law C) Bernoulli’s principle D) Archimedes’ principle

✅ Correct Answer: B) Pascal’s law

Solution:

Pascal’s law states that a pressure change at any point in an enclosed incompressible fluid is transmitted equally in all directions. This is the working principle of hydraulic systems.

14. What is the effect on the velocity of a fluid when it passes through a narrower section of a pipe (assuming streamline flow)?

A) Increases B) Decreases C) Remains the same D) Becomes zero

✅ Correct Answer: A) Increases

Solution:

According to the continuity equation (A₁V₁ = A₂V₂), as the cross-sectional area decreases, the velocity of the fluid must increase to conserve mass in steady flow.

15. The upward force acting on a body immersed in a fluid is called:

A) Weight B) Buoyant force C) Pressure D) Tension

✅ Correct Answer: B) Buoyant force

Solution:

Buoyant force is the upward force exerted by a fluid on a submerged object. It equals the weight of the fluid displaced, as stated by Archimedes’ principle.

16. The Reynolds number is used to determine:

A) Density of the fluid B) Type of fluid flow C) Viscosity of fluid D) Pressure gradient

✅ Correct Answer: B) Type of fluid flow

Solution:

The Reynolds number (Re) predicts the flow regime: laminar if Re < 2000, turbulent if Re > 4000. It depends on fluid velocity, characteristic length, density, and viscosity.

17. When a body floats in a liquid, the weight of the displaced liquid is:

A) More than the weight of the body B) Equal to the weight of the body C) Less than the weight of the body D) Cannot be determined

✅ Correct Answer: B) Equal to the weight of the body

Solution:

For a floating object, the buoyant force (equal to the weight of displaced liquid) balances the weight of the object. Hence, they are equal.

18. Which of the following quantities is NOT a scalar?

A) Speed B) Temperature C) Velocity D) Mass

✅ Correct Answer: C) Velocity

Solution:

Velocity is a vector quantity — it has both magnitude and direction. The others (speed, temperature, mass) are scalars because they only have magnitude.

19. Which instrument is used to measure atmospheric pressure?

A) Hydrometer B) Manometer C) Barometer D) Thermometer

✅ Correct Answer: C) Barometer

Solution:

A barometer is specifically designed to measure atmospheric pressure. It can be mercury-based or aneroid (mechanical). Hydrometers measure density, manometers measure pressure differences, and thermometers measure temperature.

20. Bernoulli’s principle is applicable to:

A) Rotational motion B) Viscous flow C) Turbulent flow D) Streamline flow

✅ Correct Answer: D) Streamline flow

Solution:

Bernoulli’s equation is valid only for streamline (laminar) flow of an incompressible and non-viscous fluid. It explains the inverse relation between pressure and velocity in such systems.

21. Which law explains the process of osmosis?

A) Newton’s Law B) Archimedes’ Principle C) Pascal’s Law D) None of these

✅ Correct Answer: D) None of these

Solution:

Osmosis is a biological process governed by concentration gradients and semi-permeable membranes, not described by Newton, Archimedes, or Pascal. It follows principles of diffusion rather than a specific “law” from classical physics.

22. Which gas is used in fire extinguishers?

A) Oxygen B) Carbon monoxide C) Carbon dioxide D) Nitrogen

✅ Correct Answer: C) Carbon dioxide

Solution:

CO₂ displaces oxygen and cools the flames, effectively putting out the fire without leaving a residue. It’s ideal for electrical and liquid fires.

23. The pressure at a point in a static fluid depends on:

A) Shape of the container B) Depth of the point C) Area of the fluid surface D) Volume of fluid

✅ Correct Answer: B) Depth of the point

Solution:

In static fluids, pressure increases with depth according to the formula \( P = \rho g h \), where \( \rho \) is density, \( g \) is acceleration due to gravity, and \( h \) is depth. Container shape or surface area has no effect.

24. Why does a body float in a liquid?

A) Its density is more than the liquid B) Its weight is more than upthrust C) Its density is less than the liquid D) Liquid has zero viscosity

✅ Correct Answer: C) Its density is less than the liquid

Solution:

A body floats when the buoyant force equals its weight. This occurs when its density is **less than** the density of the liquid, according to Archimedes’ Principle.

25. Bernoulli’s principle is based on the conservation of:

A) Mass B) Momentum C) Energy D) Pressure

✅ Correct Answer: C) Energy

Solution:

Bernoulli’s principle states that in a streamline flow, the sum of pressure energy, kinetic energy, and potential energy per unit volume is constant. Hence, it's based on the **conservation of mechanical energy**.
