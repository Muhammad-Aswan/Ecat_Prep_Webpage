🔥 Practice MCQs (MEASUREMENT)

1. The number of significant figures in 0.00560 is:

A) 2 B) 3 C) 4 D) 5

✅ Correct Answer: B) 3

Only digits after the leading zeros and the final zero after decimal count. So: 5, 6, 0 → 3 significant figures.

2. Which of the following physical quantities is dimensionless?

A) Force B) Strain C) Velocity D) Energy

✅ Correct Answer: B) Strain

Strain = change in length / original length → unit cancels → dimensionless.

3. The unit of Planck’s constant is:

A) kg m/s B) J C) J s D) N m

✅ Correct Answer: C) J s

E = h f ⇒ h = E / f = Joules × seconds = J s

4. Which of these has the same dimensions as impulse?

A) Energy B) Power C) Momentum D) Force

✅ Correct Answer: C) Momentum

Impulse = Force × time = (mass × acceleration) × time = mass × velocity = momentum

5. Dimensional formula of power is:

A) M L² T⁻³ B) M L² T⁻² C) M L T⁻² D) M L⁰ T⁰

✅ Correct Answer: A) M L² T⁻³

Power = Energy / Time → (M L² T⁻²) / T = M L² T⁻³

6. A measurement reads 3.00 × 10². How many significant figures are there?

A) 1 B) 2 C) 3 D) 4

✅ Correct Answer: C) 3

Zeros after decimal are significant → 3.00 → 3 significant figures

7. The derived SI unit of pressure is:

A) N B) J C) N/m² D) kg m/s²

✅ Correct Answer: C) N/m²

Pressure = Force / Area = N / m²

8. What is the dimension of universal gravitational constant G?

A) M⁻¹ L³ T⁻² B) M L³ T⁻² C) M⁻² L³ T² D) M L T⁻²

✅ Correct Answer: A) M⁻¹ L³ T⁻²

From F = G m₁m₂ / r² → G = F r² / m² = (M L T⁻²)(L²) / M² = M⁻¹ L³ T⁻²

9. Percentage uncertainty in A = 4.0 ± 0.2 is:

A) 2% B) 4% C) 5% D) 10%

✅ Correct Answer: D) 5%

Uncertainty % = (0.2 / 4.0) × 100 = 5%

10. The constant in E = mc² has which dimensions?

A) L² T⁻² B) M L² T⁻² C) L T⁻¹ D) L T

✅ Correct Answer: A) L² T⁻²

c² has units of (m/s)² → dimensions = L² T⁻²

11. Which quantity has the same dimension as work?

A) Torque B) Power C) Momentum D) Pressure

✅ Correct Answer: A) Torque

Both work and torque have dimensions of M L² T⁻².

12. In measurement, the random error can be reduced by:

A) Using better instrument B) Taking average of many readings C) Calibrating instrument D) Using digital meter

✅ Correct Answer: B) Taking average of many readings

Random errors cancel out when you average multiple readings.

13. Which one is NOT a fundamental quantity?

A) Temperature B) Time C) Pressure D) Length

✅ Correct Answer: C) Pressure

Pressure is derived from Force/Area. Others are fundamental.

14. A quantity has units kg m² s⁻². What is it?

A) Energy B) Force C) Power D) Momentum

✅ Correct Answer: A) Energy

M L² T⁻² is the unit for energy (Joules).

15. Accuracy of measurement depends on:

A) Observer skill B) Least count of instrument C) Measuring time D) Number of digits

✅ Correct Answer: B) Least count of instrument

Smaller least count = more accurate measurement.

16. 1 angstrom = ? m

A) 10⁻⁶ m B) 10⁻⁸ m C) 10⁻⁹ m D) 10⁻¹⁰ m

✅ Correct Answer: D) 10⁻¹⁰ m

It’s a standard atomic scale length unit: 1 Å = 10⁻¹⁰ meters.

17. The standard meter is defined as:

A) Distance between two marks on platinum rod B) Length of a pendulum C) Distance light travels in 1/299,792,458 seconds D) Wavelength of red light

✅ Correct Answer: C) Distance light travels in 1/299,792,458 seconds

Modern SI definition of meter is based on speed of light.

18. Which pair has same dimensional formula?

A) Power and Energy B) Impulse and Momentum C) Work and Force D) Pressure and Energy

✅ Correct Answer: B) Impulse and Momentum

Impulse = Force × time = M L T⁻¹ = Momentum

19. Which of these has different dimensions than the others?

A) Energy B) Work C) Torque D) Power

✅ Correct Answer: D) Power

Power has dimension M L² T⁻³, others are M L² T⁻²

20. Dimensional formula of Planck’s constant h is:

A) M L² T⁻² B) M L² T⁻¹ C) M L T⁻² D) M L² T⁻³

✅ Correct Answer: B) M L² T⁻¹

h = Energy / frequency = (M L² T⁻²) / T⁻¹ = M L² T⁻¹

21. Which of the following is NOT dimensionally correct?

A) Force = mass × acceleration B) Energy = force × distance C) Pressure = energy / volume D) Power = force × velocity

✅ Correct Answer: D) Power = force × velocity

Actually, this is correct. The wrong one is C – pressure is force/area, not energy/volume.

22. The SI unit for solid angle is:

A) Radian B) Steradian C) Degree D) Candela

✅ Correct Answer: B) Steradian

Steradian (sr) is used for measuring solid angles in 3D space.

23. Dimension of magnetic flux is:

A) M L² T⁻² A⁻¹ B) M L² T⁻¹ A⁻¹ C) M L² T⁻² A⁻² D) M L² T⁻³ A⁻¹

✅ Correct Answer: A) M L² T⁻² A⁻¹

Magnetic flux = B × A → Tesla × m² → (M T⁻² A⁻¹) × m² = M L² T⁻² A⁻¹

24. When two quantities are added, the result should have:

✅ Correct Answer: A) Least decimal places

Addition/subtraction rules follow least number of decimal places, not sig figs.

25. Dimensional formula of surface tension is:

A) M T⁻² B) M L T⁻² C) M T⁻² L⁻¹ D) M L² T⁻²

✅ Correct Answer: C) M T⁻² L⁻¹

Surface tension = Force / length = (M L T⁻²) / L = M T⁻² L⁻¹

1. Two forces of 7 N and 24 N act at an angle of 90° to each other. What is the magnitude of their resultant?

A) 25 N B) 17 N C) 31 N D) 13 N

✅ Correct Answer: A) 25 N

Solution:

Why we are using pythagoras therom here? Because here two parameters given and the angle is 90°, which means that triangle has one angle is 90°.
Use the Pythagorean Theorem: R = √(7² + 24²) = √(49 + 576) = √625 = 25 N
📌 Concept: Vector addition using right angle rule.

2. If the dot product of two vectors A and B is zero, and their magnitudes are non-zero, what is the angle between them?

A) 0° B) 45° C) 90° D) 180°

✅ Correct Answer: C) 90°

Solution:

TRICK: DOT PRODUCT: A · B = ABcosθ dot product is given zero means: A · B = 0
magnitudes of vectors are non zero means: AB ≠ 0
A · B = ABcosθ ==> 0 = ABcosθ ==> cos-1(0) = AB ==> 90° = AB
Cos(90) = 0, so Cos-1(0) = 90°

3. A vector of magnitude 10 units makes an angle of 60° with the x-axis. What is the Y-component of the vector?

A) 5 units B) 8.66 units C) 10 units D) 6.5 units

Solution:

TRICK: Question have only one vector and with given x-y components, it means that angle is always 60, We just have to check what to apply Sin or Cos
Formulas:
Ax = Acosθ and Ay = Asinθ
Here we will use Ay = Asinθ because we are given to find the Y-Component
So, Ay = 10Sin(60) = 10(1/√2) = 8.66 units
📌 Concept: Trigonometric resolution into components.

📌 Concept: Cross product of collinear vectors is zero.

9. If a vector A = 4i + 3j and vector B = 2i − j, what is the angle between them?

A) 30° B) 45° C) 60° D) 90°

✅ Correct Answer: C) 60°

Solution:

A · B = |A||B|cosθ
A · B = (4)(2) + (3)(−1) = 8 − 3 = 5
|A| = √(16 + 9) = √25 = 5, |B| = √(4 + 1) = √5
⇒ cosθ = 5 / (5√5) = 1/√5
⇒ θ = cos−1(1/√5) ≈ 63.4° ≈ 60°
📌 Concept: Angle via dot product & magnitude.

10. A vector makes an angle of 45° with both the x and y axes. What is the magnitude of its resultant component in the XY-plane if its magnitude is 10?

Components: Ax = 10cos45° = 10(1/√2) = 7.07, Ay = 10sin45° = 7.07

But each component is 7.07
📌 Concept: Equal angle resolution.

25. A block of mass 10 kg is pulled along a rough horizontal surface with a force of 100 N at an angle of 30° above the horizontal. The coefficient of kinetic friction is 0.4. What is the acceleration of the block?

✅ Correct Answer: A) 2.3 m/s²

Net force = Fx − friction = ~86.6 − 20 = 66.6 N
Acceleration = F / m = 66.6 / 10 ≈ 2.3 m/s²
📌 Concept: Forces on an inclined pull, vector resolution, kinetic friction.

🔥 Practice MCQs (WORK AND ENERGY)

1. Which of the following statements is correct regarding work done?

A) Work is always positive B) Work is always negative C) Work is scalar and can be positive, negative or zero D) Work is a vector

✅ Correct Answer: C) Work is scalar and can be positive, negative or zero

Solution:

Work depends on the angle between force and displacement; hence it can be positive, negative or zero. Work is a scalar quantity.

2. A body of mass 2 kg is lifted vertically through 10 m. What is the work done against gravity?

A) 10 J B) 20 J C) 196 J D) 100 J

✅ Correct Answer: C) 196 J

Solution:

Work = mgh = 2 × 9.8 × 10 = 196 J

3. Which physical quantity is the rate of doing work?

A) Energy B) Force C) Power D) Momentum

✅ Correct Answer: C) Power

Solution:

Power is defined as the rate at which work is done. Power = Work / Time.

4. A body is moving in a circular path with constant speed. What can be said about its work done by centripetal force?

A) Positive B) Negative C) Zero D) Depends on radius

✅ Correct Answer: C) Zero

Solution:

Work is zero when force is perpendicular to displacement. Since centripetal force is always perpendicular to velocity, it does no work.

5. A body of mass 2 kg falls freely from a height of 5 m. The work done by gravity is:

A) 10 J B) 50 J C) 100 J D) Zero

✅ Correct Answer: B) 50 J

Solution:
