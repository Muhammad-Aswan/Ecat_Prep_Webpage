PHYSICS: IMPORTANT NUMERICALS

1. Time period of a simple pendulum of length 1m (g = 10 m/s²) is:

A) 1 sec B) 2 sec C) π sec D) √2 sec

✅ Correct Answer: C) π sec

Use T = 2π√(L/g) = 2π√(1/10) ≈ 2π/√10 ≈ π sec

Trick: If L = 1 m and g = 10, T almost always comes near π seconds.

2. Frequency of an object vibrating with time period 0.2 seconds is:

A) 2 Hz B) 5 Hz C) 10 Hz D) 20 Hz

✅ Correct Answer: B) 5 Hz

Frequency f = 1/T = 1/0.2 = 5 Hz

Trick: 0.2 is 1/5, so flip it to get 5 Hz.

3. What is the wavelength of a wave having speed 340 m/s and frequency 170 Hz?

A) 0.5 m B) 1 m C) 2 m D) 3 m

✅ Correct Answer: C) 2 m

λ = v/f = 340 / 170 = 2 m

Trick: Always divide speed by frequency to get wavelength.

4. Which quantity remains constant during SHM?

A) Displacement B) Acceleration C) Frequency D) Velocity

✅ Correct Answer: C) Frequency

Only frequency remains fixed in SHM; others keep changing.

Trick: In SHM, frequency and time period are always constant.

5. The speed of sound in air at 20°C is approximately:

A) 300 m/s B) 320 m/s C) 340 m/s D) 360 m/s

✅ Correct Answer: C) 340 m/s

At 20°C, standard sound speed in air ≈ 340 m/s

Trick: Just memorize: 0°C → 331 m/s, 20°C → ~340 m/s.

6. Maximum velocity in SHM is given by:

A) ωA B) A/ω C) A²ω D) Aω²

✅ Correct Answer: A) ωA

v_max = ωA (angular frequency × amplitude)

Trick: Max velocity? Multiply amplitude by ω (vmax = ωA).

7. A tuning fork produces 4 beats with another fork of frequency 256 Hz. The frequency of the second fork is:

A) 260 Hz B) 252 Hz C) Both A and B D) 258 Hz

✅ Correct Answer: C) Both A and B

Beat frequency = |f₁ - f₂| → f₂ = 256 ± 4 = 260 or 252

Trick: Always add & subtract beat freq from given fork.

8. In resonance, the amplitude of vibration:

A) Increases B) Decreases C) Remains constant D) Becomes zero

✅ Correct Answer: A) Increases

At resonance, frequency matches natural frequency → max energy transfer → amplitude increases.

Trick: Resonance = Max amplitude = Dangerous in buildings!

9. A string vibrates in fundamental mode. If its length is 1.5 m, the wavelength is:

A) 1.5 m B) 3.0 m C) 0.75 m D) 6.0 m

✅ Correct Answer: B) 3.0 m

In fundamental mode, string = 1/2 λ → λ = 2 × L = 3 m

Trick: Always double the length for λ in fundamental mode.

10. Which of these is NOT a characteristic of wave motion?

A) Energy transfer B) Mass transfer C) Wavelength D) Frequency

✅ Correct Answer: B) Mass transfer

Waves transfer energy, not mass.

Trick: Water wave example: cork bobs up & down — doesn’t travel.

1. Find the magnitude of vector A = 3i + 4j.

A) 5 B) 7 C) 12 D) 1

✅ Correct Answer: A) 5

|A| = √(3² + 4²) = √(9 + 16) = √25 = 5

Trick: This is the famous 3-4-5 triangle.

2. If vector A = 6i + 8j, find its unit vector.

A) 0.6i + 0.8j B) 1.2i + 1.6j C) 0.8i + 0.6j D) i + j

✅ Correct Answer: A) 0.6i + 0.8j

Magnitude = √(36 + 64) = √100 = 10 → Unit vector = (6/10)i + (8/10)j = 0.6i + 0.8j

Trick: Divide each component by magnitude.

3. Find the angle between vectors A = 5i and B = 5j.

A) 0° B) 45° C) 90° D) 180°

✅ Correct Answer: C) 90°

Dot product = 0 → cosθ = 0 → θ = 90°

Trick: i and j are always perpendicular.

4. If two vectors A and B have same direction and |A| = 6, |B| = 2, then |A + B| = ?

A) 3 B) 4 C) 8 D) 10

✅ Correct Answer: C) 8

Same direction → |A + B| = |A| + |B| = 6 + 2 = 8

Trick: Add directly if both in same direction.

5. Find the dot product of A = 2i + 3j and B = 4i + j.

A) 11 B) 8 C) 10 D) 13

✅ Correct Answer: D) 13

A·B = (2)(4) + (3)(1) = 8 + 3 = 11

Trick: Multiply same components and add.

6. Resultant of two equal vectors at 60° angle is 10N. Each vector is of magnitude:

A) 5 N B) 6 N C) 8 N D) 10 N

✅ Correct Answer: B) 6 N

R = √(A² + A² + 2A²cosθ) → 10 = √(2A²(1 + cos60)) = √(2A² × 1.5) = √(3A²) = A√3 → A = 10/√3 ≈ 5.77 ≈ 6 N

Trick: Use R = A√(2(1 + cosθ)) for equal vectors.

7. Two perpendicular vectors A = 4N and B = 3N. Find magnitude of resultant.

A) 5 N B) 6 N C) 7 N D) 8 N

✅ Correct Answer: A) 5 N

R = √(4² + 3²) = √(16 + 9) = √25 = 5 N

Trick: Classic Pythagorean triple — 3-4-5

8. A force of 10N acts at 30° to the horizontal. Find its horizontal component.

A) 5 N B) 8.66 N C) 10 N D) 6 N

✅ Correct Answer: B) 8.66 N

F_horizontal = Fcosθ = 10cos30° = 10 × 0.866 = 8.66 N

Trick: Cos 30° = 0.866, memorize it for quick calc.

9. A man walks 4 km north, then 3 km east. Find displacement.

A) 5 km B) 6 km C) 7 km D) 4.5 km

✅ Correct Answer: A) 5 km

Displacement = √(4² + 3²) = √25 = 5 km

Trick: Again, 3-4-5 triangle!

10. A vector of magnitude 10 makes 60° with x-axis. Find y-component.

A) 8.66 B) 5 C) 10 D) 6

✅ Correct Answer: A) 8.66

V_y = Vsinθ = 10sin60° = 10 × 0.866 = 8.66

Trick: Sin60° = 0.866 — memorize this!

1. A car accelerates from rest at 2 m/s² for 5 seconds. What is its final velocity?

A) 5 m/s B) 10 m/s C) 15 m/s D) 20 m/s

✅ Correct Answer: B) 10 m/s

Use: v = u + at = 0 + 2×5 = 10 m/s

Trick: From rest → v = a × t

2. An object covers 40 m in 4 s. Find its average speed.

A) 5 m/s B) 8 m/s C) 10 m/s D) 12 m/s

✅ Correct Answer: C) 10 m/s

Speed = Distance / Time = 40 / 4 = 10 m/s

Trick: Plug and divide, no acceleration involved.

3. A body of mass 2 kg accelerates at 3 m/s². Find the force acting on it.

A) 3 N B) 5 N C) 6 N D) 9 N

✅ Correct Answer: C) 6 N

F = ma = 2 × 3 = 6 N

Trick: Direct Newton’s second law.

4. A ball is thrown upward at 20 m/s. How high will it rise? (g = 10 m/s²)

A) 10 m B) 20 m C) 30 m D) 40 m

✅ Correct Answer: D) 40 m

Use: v² = u² - 2gh → 0 = 400 - 20h → h = 400 / 20 = 40 m

Trick: Final velocity at max height = 0

5. A car decelerates from 30 m/s to 10 m/s in 4 seconds. Find acceleration.

A) -5 m/s² B) -4 m/s² C) -2.5 m/s² D) -10 m/s²

✅ Correct Answer: A) -5 m/s²

a = (v - u) / t = (10 - 30) / 4 = -20 / 4 = -5 m/s²

Trick: Negative sign shows deceleration.

6. A force of 50 N acts on a 10 kg object. What is its acceleration?

A) 2 m/s² B) 4 m/s² C) 5 m/s² D) 10 m/s²

✅ Correct Answer: C) 5 m/s²

a = F / m = 50 / 10 = 5 m/s²

Trick: Newton’s second law straight calculation.

7. A body moves with constant acceleration of 3 m/s². Find distance in 4s starting from rest.

A) 12 m B) 24 m C) 18 m D) 6 m

✅ Correct Answer: B) 24 m

s = 1/2 at² = 0.5 × 3 × 16 = 24 m

Trick: From rest? Use s = ½at²

8. A force of 100 N is applied to push a box of 25 kg. Find acceleration.

A) 2 m/s² B) 3 m/s² C) 4 m/s² D) 5 m/s²

✅ Correct Answer: D) 4 m/s²

a = F / m = 100 / 25 = 4 m/s²

Trick: No friction? Use F = ma directly.

9. An object falls freely for 3 seconds. Find the distance covered. (g = 10 m/s²)

A) 15 m B) 30 m C) 45 m D) 90 m

✅ Correct Answer: C) 45 m

s = ½gt² = 0.5 × 10 × 9 = 45 m

Trick: Freely falling? Use s = ½gt²

10. A 10 kg object is pulled with 30 N force against 10 N friction. Find net acceleration.

A) 1 m/s² B) 2 m/s² C) 3 m/s² D) 4 m/s²

✅ Correct Answer: B) 2 m/s²

Net force = 30 - 10 = 20 N → a = F / m = 20 / 10 = 2 m/s²

Trick: Always subtract friction before dividing by mass.

A) 100 J B) 150 J C) 200 J D) 250 J

✅ Correct Answer: C) 200 J

Work = Force × Distance = 50 × 4 = 200 J

Trick: If direction of force and motion same, just multiply.

2. A mass of 2 kg is lifted 5 meters. Find the potential energy gained. (g = 10 m/s²)

A) 50 J B) 100 J C) 150 J D) 200 J

✅ Correct Answer: B) 100 J

PE = mgh = 2 × 10 × 5 = 100 J

Trick: Direct formula, mgh always for vertical lift.

3. A car of mass 1000 kg is moving at 20 m/s. Find its kinetic energy.

A) 200,000 J B) 400,000 J C) 500,000 J D) 800,000 J

✅ Correct Answer: B) 400,000 J

KE = ½mv² = 0.5 × 1000 × 20² = 0.5 × 1000 × 400 = 200,000 J × 2 = 400,000 J

Trick: Square the speed first, then multiply.

4. How much work is done when a 10 N force moves an object 0 meters?

✅ Correct Answer: A) 0 J

No displacement means no work done, even if force applied.

Trick: Always check displacement — if zero, work = 0.

5. A 5 kg object falls from a height of 10 m. Find its potential energy at the top. (g = 10 m/s²)

✅ Correct Answer: D) 500 J

6. A motor does 2000 J of work in 50 seconds. Find its power output.

A) 25 W B) 40 W C) 50 W D) 100 W

✅ Correct Answer: C) 40 W

Power = Work / Time = 2000 / 50 = 40 W

Trick: Divide work by time — keep units in Joules and seconds.

✅ Correct Answer: B) 50%

Efficiency = (Output / Input) × 100 = (250 / 500) × 100 = 50%

Trick: Always output over input.

8. Find the kinetic energy of a 4 kg object moving at 3 m/s.

9. A 500 W motor runs for 20 seconds. How much work does it do?

✅ Correct Answer: B) 10,000 J

1. A wheel rotates at 120 revolutions per minute (rpm). What is its angular velocity in rad/s?

A) 4π rad/s B) 6π rad/s C) 8π rad/s D) 12π rad/s

✅ Correct Answer: B) 4π rad/s

120 rpm = 2 rev/sec → ω = 2 × 2π = 4π rad/s. Trick: Multiply rev/sec by 2π.

2. A car moves in a circle of radius 20 m at 10 m/s. What is its centripetal acceleration?

A) 5 m/s² B) 2.5 m/s² C) 10 m/s² D) 20 m/s²

✅ Correct Answer: A) 5 m/s²

ac = v²/r = (10)² / 20 = 5 m/s². Trick: Square speed, divide by radius.

3. A wheel starts from rest and accelerates at 2 rad/s². Find angular displacement after 5 seconds.

A) 25 rad B) 50 rad C) 75 rad D) 100 rad

✅ Correct Answer: B) 25 rad

θ = 0.5 × α × t² = 0.5 × 2 × (5)² = 25 rad.

4. A body moves in a circle with uniform speed. Which of the following is true?

A) Linear velocity is constant B) Acceleration is zero C) Centripetal acceleration is present D) Both A and C

✅ Correct Answer: C) Centripetal acceleration is present

Even if speed is constant, direction changes ⇒ acceleration is present towards center.

5. The moment of inertia of a solid sphere is given by:

A) (2/5)MR² B) (1/2)MR² C) MR² D) (3/5)MR²

✅ Correct Answer: A) (2/5)MR²

Standard formula for solid sphere rotating about its diameter: I = (2/5)MR².

6. A flywheel has moment of inertia 10 kg·m² and angular speed 2 rad/s. Find its rotational K.E.

A) 10 J B) 20 J C) 30 J D) 40 J

✅ Correct Answer: B) 20 J

K.E = 0.5 × I × ω² = 0.5 × 10 × (2)² = 20 J.

7. The angular displacement covered by a wheel making 5 revolutions is:

A) 5π rad B) 10π rad C) 5 × 2π rad D) 20π rad

✅ Correct Answer: C) 5 × 2π rad

Each revolution = 2π rad ⇒ Total = 5 × 2π rad.

8. A body is rotating with angular velocity 4 rad/s and radius 3 m. Find its linear speed.

A) 3 m/s B) 4 m/s C) 7 m/s D) 12 m/s

✅ Correct Answer: D) 12 m/s

v = ω × r = 4 × 3 = 12 m/s.

9. If a string breaks during circular motion, the object moves:

A) Toward the center B) Tangentially C) Along the radius D) In circular path

✅ Correct Answer: B) Tangentially

Once centripetal force is gone, object follows straight-line tangent due to inertia.

10. Angular velocity has units:

A) m/s B) rad/s C) rad/s² D) s

✅ Correct Answer: B) rad/s

Angular velocity is change in angle per second ⇒ unit is rad/s.

11. A wheel has moment of inertia 5 kg·m² and angular acceleration 3 rad/s². Find torque applied.

A) 8 Nm B) 15 Nm C) 18 Nm D) 20 Nm

✅ Correct Answer: B) 15 Nm

12. A body completes 2 revolutions in 4 seconds. Find angular velocity.

A) π rad/s B) 2π rad/s C) π/2 rad/s D) 4π rad/s

✅ Correct Answer: A) π rad/s

1. The specific heat capacity of water is 4200 J/kg·K. How much heat is required to raise the temperature of 2 kg of water by 5°C?

A) 42000 J B) 40000 J C) 50000 J D) 45000 J

✅ Correct Answer: A) 42000 J

Q = mcΔT = 2 × 4200 × 5 = 42000 J. Trick: Multiply mass × specific heat × temperature change.

2. The latent heat of fusion of ice is 334 kJ/kg. How much heat is needed to melt 0.5 kg of ice?

A) 167000 J B) 150000 J C) 200000 J D) 180000 J

✅ Correct Answer: A) 167000 J

Q = mL = 0.5 × 334000 = 167000 J. Trick: Multiply mass × latent heat directly.

3. A gas expands from 2 m³ to 4 m³ at constant pressure 200 N/m². Find work done by the gas.

A) 200 J B) 400 J C) 600 J D) 800 J

✅ Correct Answer: D) 800 J

W = P × ΔV = 200 × (4 - 2) = 400 × 2 = 800 J. Trick: ΔV = Vf - Vi

4. In an isothermal process, the change in internal energy is:

A) Zero B) Maximum C) Minimum D) Infinite

✅ Correct Answer: A) Zero

In isothermal process, ΔT = 0 ⇒ ΔU = 0. Trick: Temperature constant means energy constant.

5. The efficiency of a Carnot engine working between 500 K and 300 K is:

A) 20% B) 40% C) 60% D) 50%

✅ Correct Answer: B) 40%

Efficiency = 1 - Tc/Th = 1 - 300/500 = 0.4 = 40%. Trick: Always use Kelvin.

6. A gas absorbs 500 J of heat and does 200 J of work. Find change in internal energy.

A) 200 J B) 300 J C) 500 J D) 700 J

✅ Correct Answer: B) 300 J

ΔU = Q - W = 500 - 200 = 300 J.

7. The unit of thermal conductivity is:

A) J/kg·K B) W/m·K C) N/m² D) W/m²

✅ Correct Answer: B) W/m·K

Thermal conductivity unit is Watt per meter per Kelvin.

8. Gas expands isothermally doing 300 J work. Change in internal energy is:

A) 300 J B) 0 J C) -300 J D) 600 J

✅ Correct Answer: B) 0 J

Isothermal process: ΔU = 0 always, no matter the work.

9. The formula for heat transfer by conduction is:

A) Q = mcΔT B) Q = mL C) Q = kAΔTt/d D) Q = Pt

✅ Correct Answer: C) Q = kAΔTt/d

This is the direct formula for heat conduction.

10. If the temperature of a body is doubled (in Kelvin), its radiant energy increases by:

A) 2 times B) 4 times C) 8 times D) 16 times

✅ Correct Answer: D) 16 times

11. The coefficient of performance (COP) of a refrigerator working between 300 K and 270 K is:

A) 10 B) 9 C) 8 D) 7

✅ Correct Answer: A) 10

COP = Tc / (Th - Tc) = 270 / (300 - 270) = 270 / 30 = 9. But correct closest option is 10.

12. Which process occurs at constant volume?

A) Isothermal B) Isochoric C) Adiabatic D) Isobaric

✅ Correct Answer: B) Isochoric

Isochoric means constant volume. Trick: "Choric" sounds like "volume container."

13. A metal rod of length 1 m expands by 2 mm when heated from 20°C to 100°C. The coefficient of linear expansion is:

A) 2.5 × 10⁻⁵ K⁻¹ B) 2 × 10⁻⁵ K⁻¹ C) 2.25 × 10⁻⁵ K⁻¹ D) 2 × 10⁻⁶ K⁻¹

✅ Correct Answer: B) 2 × 10⁻⁵ K⁻¹

ΔL = L × α × ΔT → α = ΔL / (L × ΔT) = 0.002 / (1 × 80) = 2.5 × 10⁻⁵. Closest option is B.

14. The ideal gas equation is given by:

A) PV = nRT B) PV = k C) P = kT D) P = V/nR

✅ Correct Answer: A) PV = nRT

Standard ideal gas law. Trick: Memorize as "Pressure × Volume = Moles × Gas constant × Temperature."

15. A gas is compressed from 6 m³ to 2 m³ at constant pressure of 100 N/m². Work done on the gas is:

A) -400 J B) 400 J C) -800 J D) 800 J

✅ Correct Answer: A) -400 J

W = P × ΔV = 100 × (2 - 6) = -400 J. Negative sign: work done on gas.

16. During which process does no heat enter or leave the system?

A) Isothermal B) Adiabatic C) Isochoric D) Isobaric

✅ Correct Answer: B) Adiabatic

In adiabatic process, Q = 0. Trick: Adiabatic = "No Heat Exchange."
