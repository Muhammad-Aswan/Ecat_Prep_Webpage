PHYSICS: IMPORTANT FORMULAS

📚 Complete Physics Formula Sheet (Quick Explanation)

1. Motion

  • v = u + at — Final velocity after time t
  • s = ut + ½at² — Displacement after time t
  • v² = u² + 2as — Relates velocity & displacement
  • a = (v - u) / t — Acceleration formula

2. Force & Laws of Motion

  • F = ma — Force equals mass × acceleration
  • P = F/A — Pressure = Force per unit area
  • Impulse = F × Δt = Δp — Change in momentum

3. Work, Power & Energy

  • Work = F × d × cosθ — Work done by force at angle θ
  • K.E = ½mv² — Kinetic Energy of moving object
  • P.E = mgh — Potential Energy at height h
  • Power = Work / time — Rate of doing work

4. Circular Motion

  • v = rω — Linear speed in circular path
  • a = v²/r = rω² — Centripetal acceleration
  • T = 2πr / v — Time period for one revolution

5. Gravitation

  • F = Gm₁m₂ / r² — Gravitational force between masses
  • g = GM / R² — Acceleration due to gravity
  • P.E = -GMm / r — Gravitational Potential Energy

6. Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM)

  • x = A sin(ωt) — Displacement at time t
  • ω = 2π / T — Angular frequency
  • T = 2π√(m/k) — Time period of spring
  • T = 2π√(L/g) — Time period of pendulum

7. Heat & Thermodynamics

  • Q = mcΔT — Heat energy change
  • PV = nRT — Ideal Gas Equation
  • W = PΔV — Work done by expanding gas
  • Efficiency = (Work Output / Heat Input) × 100%

8. Optics (Light)

  • v = u + 2f — Mirror/Lens formula (approximate shortcut)
  • 1/f = 1/v - 1/u — Mirror/Lens formula (actual)
  • m = -v/u — Magnification
  • n₁ sinθ₁ = n₂ sinθ₂ — Snell's Law of refraction
  • Critical angle: sinC = n₂ / n₁

9. Current Electricity

  • V = IR — Ohm's Law
  • P = VI = I²R = V²/R — Power formulas
  • R = ρL/A — Resistance formula
  • Q = It — Charge flow over time

10. Magnetism & Electromagnetism

  • B = μ₀I / 2πr — Magnetic field due to long wire
  • F = qvB sinθ — Magnetic force on moving charge
  • F = ILB sinθ — Force on current-carrying conductor
  • Torque = NIBA sinθ — Torque on current loop

11. Electromagnetic Induction

  • e = -dΦ/dt — Faraday's Law (induced emf)
  • Φ = BA cosθ — Magnetic flux
  • e = Blv — Motional emf

12. Modern Physics

  • E = hf — Energy of photon
  • E = mc² — Mass-energy equivalence
  • K.E (max) = hf - φ — Photoelectric equation
