PHYSICS: IMPORTANT NUMERICALS

Magnetic Field - Complete Chapter Summary

🔹 What is Magnetic Field?
The region around a magnet or current-carrying conductor where magnetic effects are felt. Represented by B (Tesla).

🔹 Magnetic Field Due to Current:

  • Straight Wire: B = μ₀ I / (2πr)
  • Circular Loop (center): B = μ₀ I / (2R)
  • n Loops: B = μ₀ n I / (2R)
  • Solenoid (inside): B = μ₀ n I (Uniform Field)

🔹 Force on Moving Charge in Magnetic Field:
F = q v B sinθ
Direction: Right-hand rule (fingers = velocity, palm = B-field, thumb = force).

🔹 Force on Current-Carrying Wire:
F = I L B sinθ

🔹 Charged Particle in Circular Path:

  • Radius: r = (m v) / (q B)
  • Time Period: T = 2πm / (q B)

🔹 Ampere’s Circuital Law:
∮ B · dl = μ₀ Ienclosed

🔹 Quick Magnetic Field Strength Reference:

  • Earth ≈ 10⁻⁵ T
  • Fridge Magnet ≈ 10⁻² T
  • Electromagnet ≈ 1-2 T
  • MRI ≈ 1.5 - 3 T

💡 Trick to Remember:
Where there's current, there's a magnetic field. Use your right hand for directions, and don't mix up angles in formulas!
